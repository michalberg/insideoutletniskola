## Foundational Texts

### On Pedagogy & Learning

- **John Dewey** — *Experience and Education* (1938)
  The classic argument for experiential, student-centred learning that continues to shape progressive education worldwide.

- **Paulo Freire** — *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* (1968)
  A landmark critique of the "banking model" of education; essential reading for anyone working towards transformative pedagogy.

- **bell hooks** — *Teaching to Transgress: Education as the Practice of Freedom* (1994)
  An engaged, personal account of teaching as a site of liberation, joy, and community.

- **Ken Robinson** — *Creative Schools: Revolutionizing Education from the Ground Up* (2015)
  An accessible and energetic case for rethinking what schools are for and how they can nurture human potential.

### On Democratic Education

- **A.S. Neill** — *Summerhill School* (1960)
  The original account of one of the world's most radical democratic schools — still provocative and worth arguing with.

- **Alfie Kohn** — *Punished by Rewards* (1993)
  A research-based challenge to extrinsic motivation and competitive ranking in schools and workplaces.
